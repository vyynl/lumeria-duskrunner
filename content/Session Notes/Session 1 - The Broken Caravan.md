The tale begins—how else?—in a forest.  
Not the friendly kind. The sort where roots twist like sleeping beasts, and the night presses close, as if listening.

Four [[Duskrunners]], cloaked and quiet, followed the path where an [[Ironroot Corporation]] caravan had gone missing. Now, say what you will about the Corporation and its love of timber and tyranny, but when they send spears after their vanished wagons, it tends to mean something's gone _very_ wrong—or very valuable.

They found the wreck soon enough. And it found them back.

A tripwire sang. Shadows stirred. And suddenly, seven assailants fell upon them like thunder out of still air. The attackers bore the emblem of [[The Ashened Hand]], a name spoken with reverence by some, revulsion by others. Rebels, freedom-fighters, saboteurs—depends on where you stand when the shouting starts.

The fight was fierce but brief. Five fell. Two fled. The Duskrunners gave chase, and when the dust settled, they had in their hands a captured soldier, a strange pulsing crystal, and a name that would echo for sessions to come: [[Toma]], a sergeant of the Hand. Fierce, proud, and—perhaps—surprisingly human.

They let her go.

Why? Well. That’s a tale in itself, and one not yet fully told.

**Drina**, their paladin—heart-first and steel-bound—insisted they investigate whispers of nearby ruins. What they found was a hidden Hand outpost. And two more rebels, one of them Toma’s husband.

There was talk. And for a moment, it almost held.  
But the tension snapped like bowstring under strain, and the ruin claimed two more lives.

In the silence that followed, the rebels left the Duskrunners with more than bloodshed. A name. A place. [[Nireth]]. _The Dancing Owlbear_. Someone who might hold answers—or at least the next step down a very tangled path.

From the ruin, the party also freed [[Illna]], a young girl from Wellspring, weary and wide-eyed, her magic like a song still half-sung. She left with them. Perhaps not yet a friend. But no longer a prisoner.

When they returned to the caravan wreck, the strange crystal thrummed in answer to something buried in the scorched remains. They pulled free a jagged shard of a crate, its interior marked by fading containment seals. The sort meant to keep something in.

They also gathered five Ashened Hand patches from the dead. Souvenirs. Warnings.

The light never returned to that clearing.

But the story followed them home.
