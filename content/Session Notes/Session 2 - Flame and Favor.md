The Duskrunners returned to Wellspring beneath the gleam of the shard’s false dawn—**four had left, and six returned.** Not many notice such things. But **Arannis**, ever-watchful and smugly amused, was waiting at the gate with arms crossed and one brow raised.

“Back already?” he said, with a glance at the unfamiliar faces. “Strange... most don’t return from the wastes at all—let alone with _company._”

There was some banter, a shared smirk, and then he waved them on. No accusations. No reports.

But there was weight in that look.  

Their first steps took them to [[Hearthgrove]], where the air hangs heavy with soot, sweat, and the slow desperation of those born without coin or favor. There, led by [[Illna]]’s half-remembered directions, the party found her family in a crumbling townhouse patched with love and not much else.

They were met by [[Kellen]], a halfling with a sharp wit and loving dulled by worry; [[Eldeth]], his [[Sylari]] wife with quiet eyes and a past not nearly as simple as her “tailor” title would suggest; and [[Darsis]], their young son, who clung to Illna like she might vanish again if he blinked. The reunion was tearful, relieved, and far too brief.

Their arrival had not gone unnoticed.

A cloaked figure trailing them through the streets was swiftly apprehended by the party outside the home. His green-hooded garb marked him as [[Bronze Gauntlet]], a mercenary order notorious for doing Ironroot’s dirty work when the guard preferred to look elsewhere. He didn't get far before he was unconscious.

So, with one mystery deepening, the party turned toward another.

**The Dancing Owlbear** greeted them like a tired sigh—faded wood, heavier silences, and a barkeep who didn’t ask questions unless you knew which ones to whisper. **Roondar**, ever the bold and curious scholar, asked after [[Nireth]]. The room cooled. But eventually, a false wall cracked open behind the hearth, revealing the flickering heart of a secret shrine to [[Sael]].

Down in the quiet, they met Nireth properly - a wary soul caught between loyalty and survival. She confirmed their suspicions: magic-wielders were vanishing from Hearthgrove, taken by forces no one else dared name aloud. She wanted no part in [[The Ashened Hand]]’s coming storm - too reckless, too blind in their devotion to Sael, heedless of the destruction they leave behind.

While the others spoke, **Toro** wandered to the shrine’s central flame. It flickered, but something in it was wrong. Hollow. Cold at the core. This was not the true flame he sought. The blind priestess who watched over the shrine said little, but enough. He swore to bring her back a whole flame one day.

And she believed him.

Business called once more. The party made their way to the **[[Duskrunner]] Guildhall**, past the radiant fountain that scattered light like armor against the endless dark. Inside, they found [[Varka]], a clerk of legendary indifference - until the name “Ironroot” was mentioned. Then, suddenly, a private room was available.

There, they met [[Mardred]], the guild’s liaison to the [[Ironroot Corporation]] - a silk-voiced man soaked in perfume and self importance. The party offered a tidy version of events, keeping the crystal they now hold and Illna’s rescue unspoken. Mardred thanked them with a purse and a warning wrapped in pleasantries: “Thank you for your future discretion.”

And then, of course, came [[Sorcyl]].

Draped in blue and bad taste, Sorcyl claimed to be another of Ironroot’s agents, bearing a bonus on their behalf. The party refused whatever he was selling - tone, intent, or simply his entire presence. But before they left him crying on the paving, he left them with a deed.

To [[Rootspindle House]].

A gift, he called it.

From a Corporation that never gives without reason.
