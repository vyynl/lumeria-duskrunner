## Linguistic Foundation: Arabic + Hebrew Influences

Aasimar names reflect a divine heritage rooted in sanctity, grief, and spiritual roles. Crecheborn names are sacred titles given through rite. Veiled Aasimar often modify or obscure their names for safety and autonomy.

---

## Pronunciation Key

|Symbol|Sound|Example|Notes|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Ā**|Long "a" as in _father_|_Āsīm_|Drawn-out, formal|
|**Ī**|Long "ee" as in _machine_|_Rūhī_|High vowel, sacred connotation|
|**Ū**|Long "oo" as in _rule_|_Zamrūn_|Deepened, round vowel|
|**Ḥ**|Harsh "h" from deep in throat|_Ḥakim_|Voiceless pharyngeal (like Arabic ح)|
|**Ṣ**|Hard "s" with tongue retracted|_Ṣamir_|Emphatic “s” (like Arabic ص)|
|**Ṭ**|Hard "t" with retracted tongue|_Ṭahir_|Emphatic “t” (like Arabic ط)|
|**Ḍ**|Heavy "d", tongue on roof of mouth|_Ḍamir_|Emphatic “d” (like Arabic ض)|
|**Ẓ**|Voiced “th” with tension|_Ẓahir_|Less common, poetic|
|**ʿ**|Glottal constriction|_ʿAzar_|Like Arabic ع (ʿayn), throat pop|
|**ʾ**|Glottal stop|_ʾIlan_|Break like “uh-oh”|
|**Ë / Ï / Ü**|Soft breath or identity emphasis|_Ëlah_, _Ïshem_|Veiled/formal split indicator|

## 🛠 Name Construction Tips

- For **Crecheborn**: Roll both tables, assign accents based on status and rite
    
- For **Veiled**: Roll once, then soften consonants and simplify vowels to reflect the shift toward modern, assimilated identity
