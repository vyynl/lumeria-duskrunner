## Hope and Fear
Hope and Fear are the unseen forces that shape every roll of the die. They rise and fall as the story unfolds, giving players the chance to act daringly while giving the GM the tools to press back with danger.

At the table, **Hope belongs to the players** and **Fear belongs to the GM**. Both fade at the end of each encounter.
### Rolling with Hope and Fear
Every **d20** roll carries either *Hope* or *Fear*.
- *Even* rolls resolve with **Hope**.
- *Odd* rolls resolve with **Fear**.
- Extreme results override parity:
  - Margin ≥ **+10** → always **Hope**.
  - Margin ≤ **−10** → always **Fear**.
#### Success
- **Success with Hope** *(yes, and...)*: You succeed and gain **1 Hope**. You describe how things turn out slightly better than expected.
- **Success with Fear** *(yes, but...)*: You succeed, but the GM gains **1 Fear** and describes the complication that comes with it.
#### Failure  
- **Failure with Hope** *(no, but...)*: You fail, but gain **1 Hope**. The GM describes how the outcome is softened.
- **Failure with Fear** *(no, and...)*: You fail, and the GM gains **1 Fear**. The GM describes how things worsen.

## Critical Rolls
Any **d20** roll - attack, skill check, or saving throw - can have a critical outcome.

> [!example]  Ruling
> - A natural **20** is a **Critical Success**.
> - A natural **1** is a **Critical Failure**.
> - Beating a DC by **10 or more** is also a **Critical Success**.
> - Falling short of a DC by **10 or more** is also a **Critical Failure**.

Critical results override Hope/Fear parity, but Hope and Fear may still be gained or spent.

## Rolling with Emphasis
When rolling with Emphasis, roll **2d20** and select the result farthest from 10.

> [!example] Ruling  
> - Only modifiers and proficiency apply—advantage or disadvantage never stacks with Emphasis.
> - The GM may spend Fear to impose Emphasis, representing heightened tension.

---
## Spending Hope
Each player may hold up to **3 Hope**. Hope is personal and cannot be shared.

> [!tip]  Uses
> - **Tip the Scales (1 Hope):** Attempt a bold or cinematic stunt. If it reflects your character’s experience, add **+2** to the roll.
> - **Help (1 Hope):** Aid an ally. They gain **Advantage**. This replaces the normal Help action.
> - **Tag-Team Attack (3 Hope):** Join another character in a combined strike. Both roll an attack (casters may use spell attack). Take the higher roll. On a hit, both roll damage and share the spotlight.

---

## Spending Fear
The GM may hold up to **6 Fear** at a time. Fear fuels danger, escalation, and control of the scene.

> [!danger]  
> - **Complication (1 Fear):** Introduce a new obstacle or twist.
> - **Escalation (1 Fear):** Seize or retain the spotlight for **1 additional action**.
> - **Pressure (1 Fear):** Impose a Roll with Emphasis.
> - **Dire Circumstances (3 Fear):** Trigger a dramatic shift—an ultimate attack, reinforcements, or overwhelming hazard.

---
## The Spotlight
Combat begins with initiative. The roll only decides whether the players or the GM begins with the spotlight.

- The GM takes the spotlight when:
  - A player rolls with Fear,
  - A player fails a roll,
  - A player’s action has immediate consequences, or
  - A player looks to the GM for what happens next.

> [!info] Additional Info
> - The GM spotlight lasts for **one free rollable action**.
> - To keep the spotlight, the GM must spend **1 Fear per additional action**.
> - Each enemy or hazard can only be spotlighted **once per round** unless a special ability states otherwise.
> - Without Fear to spend, the spotlight always returns to the players after the GM’s **one free action**.

## Attacks of Opportunity
To encourage movement and positioning:

> [!definition]  
> If a creature leaves melee range while surrounded by **3 or more** hostile opponents, half of those opponents (rounded down) may immediately use their reactions to make a melee attack.

---

## Serious Injury
When a character fails their 3rd death saving throw, they fall unconscious and unresponsive until the party rests.

> [!example] Ruling
> After a rest, the character revives with a **Serious Injury** debuff reflective of the harm sustained.

Examples:
- Reduced movement speed (–5 ft.) from a leg injury.
- Decreased effective range with bows or crossbows from an eye injury.

They recover to half HP after a short rest or full HP after a long rest, carrying the Serious Injury.

> [!warning]  
> A character can only sustain 1–2 Serious Injuries. Further injuries risk permanent death, barring rare and costly revivification.
