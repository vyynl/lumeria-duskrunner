## Fundamental Principle

> *"Magic is the shape your soul carves in the Aether."*
>    Dros Semithan
>      *Aetheric Consistency Across Mass Thoughtforms*

Your class *(or classes)* determine how you interact with the [[Aether]]. You don't *worship* the gods, the echo of your will feeds their forms. Every spell, power, or strike you make is a ripple in the mirror — and the pattern of that ripple is shaped by you and your class identity.

Few are able to channel the [[Aether]], but those who are have access to a well of near-limitless power if properly harnessed.

---
## Overview For Each Class
### #Barbarian: The Crack in the Mirror

> *"You do not shape the Aether. You tear through it."*

You are **rupture incarnate**. Your rate is not emotion, it is the *raw, unfiltered will to survive and destroy*. So primal that it sears the [[Aether]] wherever you go.

You presence creates unstable echoes, zone of instinctual warping where spells falter, illusions dissolve, and divine aspect recoil. Totem paths or primal ancestries often reflect *unspoken godforms* — pre-divine or proto-mythic beings who never became gods, but were burned into the [[Aether]] by sheer presence.

Some barbarians develop *battle trances* where thy glimpse older versions of themselves, or ancestral archetypes left behind in the cracked mirror.

---
### #Bard: The Composer of Form

> *"You conduct the gods like symphonies."*

You shape the [[Aether]] through your art and tales — you don't reflect an aspect; you **sample them**.

Dangerously flexible, your bardic spells are performances *inspired by* resonances of the divine, not *bound by them*. Your songs tease at [[Theryn]], [[Nystra]], or [[Sael]] without fully belonging to any — sometimes causing *divine feedback* or *awakening aspects of old* if you improvise too well.

Some bardic colleges are the secret lore-keepers, collecting the forms of forgotten gods — aspects no longer worshiped, but still faintly echoing.

---
### #Cleric: The Embodied Aspect

> *"You are the echo made flesh."*

You channel the will of the gods directly, not as supplicant, but as **embodied resonance**. The strength of your prayers *mold reality in your god's image*.

When you cast, the **aspect of your chosen god manifests** — shaped partially by you, partially by the weight of the faithful who come before. Your power waxes and wanes based on **the strength of your belief in alignment to your chosen god's form** — purity, contradiction, and even rebellion all leaving a unique echo.

You are both ***servant*** and ***shaper***.

---
### #Druid: The Rhythm of Nature

> *"You remember the shape of the world before mirrors."*

You interact with the [[Aether]] not through desire, but through the **rhythm of nature** — the primal memory of the world as it was, and could be.

Your divine alignment shifts seasonally or regionally — you may find [[Theryn]] in a sprouting seed, [[Nystra]] in the bones of a predator, or [[Sael]] in the crackling of a campfire. Rather than being rooted in your own desire, your attunement to the [[Aether]] **resonates with the world itself**, bending nature to the rhythm of your call.

Aether storms may warp your wild shape into things that have never been born or given shape.

---
### #Fighter: The Echo-Forged Self

> *"You are the consequence that others try to deny."*

You may not channel the [[Aether]] through direct casting, but your mastery of cause and effect — **the fundamental structure of combat** — creates *predictable ripples in the mirror*.

Your attunement to the fight that the [[Aether]] *aligns with your intent without invocation*, a kind of passive resonance. Some elite fighters are able to develop *echo styles* — ingraining the flow of the fight so deeply that they can *feel* the battle and react before it happens.

The Battle Master's maneuvers and Rune Knight's glyphs are examples of conscious aetheric interaction brought forth through discipline and studied tradition.

---
### #Monk: The Body That Knows

> *"Your form is your prayer."*

You are most directly tuned to the [[Aether]] through **physical discipline** — every movement becomes a glyph, every breath a stabilizing pulse.

As a monk, your martial arts are not merely combat forms, they are **rituals**, echoing divine rhythms passed down through lineages or discovered in meditation. You are able to enter **resonant states** where the boundary between material and mirrored-self becomes porous, allowing for momentary **clarity, speed,** or **intangibility.**

Ki is not just energy — it is *personal will refined into momentary perfection*.

---
### #Paladin: The Oath Brought Forth

> *"You swore, and the world answered."*

You do not channel the gods, you **bind** them through the sheer strength of your vow.

You are a weapon of belief, aetheric resonance being rooted in your **conviction**. If your belief cracks, so does your connection to your well of power. The aspect of your god does not appear to you as guidance, it appears as **judgment** or **confirmation**.

Fallen paladins will often leave behind withered fragments of their echoed vow remaining after their death — some whisper. Some scream.

---
### #Ranger: The Silent Caller

> *"You cast only when you must. But the Aether always listens."*

Your bond with nature connects you to **echoes of old divine rhythms**, but at a distance — yours is a quiet, subtle resonance.

You interact with divine pattern through **action** rather than language — hunting, protecting, surviving. Some believe the first Rangers were *aetheric guardians*, boundary-keepers between material and mirrored worlds.

Your magic may drift across godlines, responding to immediate need over long-term alignment.

---
### #Rogue: The Unwritten Echo

> *"You move where even the gods forget to look."*

You manipulate the [[Aether]] through **absence**. You don't strike from the shadows; you *create shadow* through clever defiance of expectation.

You excel at breaking echo-chains, severing cause and effect with trickery, timing, and precision. Your sneak attack is not just precision, it is a *punctuation* in the aether, speaking *"this was the moment that mattered."* to the world.

Arcane Trickers and Soulknife rogues interact more directly with the [[Aether]], *rewriting reality* or *weaponizing thought*. Some believe the greatest thieves and assassins are not just avoiding notice, but are making themselves *partially unrecorded by the mirror*.

---
### #Sorcerer: The Living Glyph

> *"You were born with a name already written in the mirror."*

You do not use the [[Aether]], you are **of** it. Your bloodline, trauma, or soul carries a *native resonance* that naturally shapes reality around you.

The god-aspects you channel are unbidden, naturally drawn to the reflection of your tuned heart. Over time, your power may evolve to reflect a **personal godform** — a private presence in the [[Aether]] blossoming from your harnessing of magic.

You may be unpredictably affected by divine imbalance, especially if your resonance overlaps with the deity most-potent in a charged location.

---
### #Warlock: The Interloper's Pact

> *"Where others ask the mirror to reflect their truth, you ask it to show someone else — and pay the price to borrow their shadow."*

You draw your power from that of another — reaching across into the domain of something vast, *not quite divine, not quite alive*.

Your patron may be a powerful mage in the [[Material]], a cast-off fragment of divinity, a pre-[[Nightfall]] relic, or a concept given form. Your pact bypasses traditional echo-formation, drawing your power from the reserve of someone/something else through a binding contract at some cost to you.

Though the power can be great with so little effort, so is the cost to the wielder...

---
### #Wizard: The Architect of Echo

> *"You diagram the divine."*

You study the Aether as a structural entity — a **metaphysical grammar** of meaning and pattern.

Rather than the gods being deities to worship, they are distinct reservoirs of power to be charted and harnessed. As a wizard, you craft your magic through rigorous study of the [[Aether]], crafting bridges to shape and meld the forces of the other realm. Your strength is not found in your will, but your knowledge of the other plane and your experience shaping the flow of energy to your will.
