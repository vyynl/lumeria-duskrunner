**Nightfall** is the name given to the apocalyptic event that marked the end of the natural cycle in Lumeria. On the final day, the sun set as it always had — there were no omens, no great storms or seismic shifts. It dipped below the horizon, and then… it did not rise again.

What followed was not chaos in a single moment, but the slow, suffocating realization that dawn would not come.

---
## Known Facts

Despite centuries of speculation, no definitive cause of Nightfall has ever been agreed upon. What can be stated with certainty:

- The sun set on the final day without incident.
- No sunrise followed.
- The stars remained visible, but the sky remained static.
- Natural warmth and crop cycles faltered over the following weeks.
- Nyxwraiths began to appear within the first sunless cycles.

While theories abound — from divine silence to failed celestial rites or hidden wars between unseen powers — there are no universally accepted records detailing what actually happened in the final days before Nightfall.

---
## The First Years

Panic did not arrive immediately. People lit lanterns and prayed for dawn. Cities held vigils. But as weeks turned into months, and no sun graced the horizon, reality set in. Crops failed. Frost crept where it did not belong. Shadows began to linger in unnatural ways. Strange reports circulated — of creatures stalking the dark, of voices whispering in the void.

These would be the first sightings of the [[Nyxwraiths]], beings born not merely of darkness, but of its permanence.

---
## The Rise of Shards and Holds

As the long dark set in, order fractured — but did not fully collapse.

In the vacuum left by fallen kingdoms and silent gods, powerful settlements consolidated into sovereign powers now known as #Holds: massive, fortified cities that act as the political, military, and arcane centers of their surrounding regions. Holds govern territory, issue charter rights, and deploy resources to sustain their influence — often through a combination of ideological appeal and monopolized control of light-based infrastructure.

Beyond the walls of the Holds lie the #Shards — smaller, self-contained settlements that persist under the protection, influence, or allegiance of a particular Hold. While some Shards operate with limited independence, most rely on trade routes, military escorts, and ideological alignment with a Hold to endure. Shards are where most of [[Lumeria]]’s population now lives: condensed communities built around stabilized light sources, clustered where the dark has been pushed back just far enough to allow breath and growth.

The relationship between Hold and Shard is not always peaceful. Some Shards chafe under tithe, taxation, or enforced doctrine. Others fiercely defend their affiliation, seeing it as the only shield between their people and annihilation.

---
## The Nyxwraith Threat

Without daylight to scatter them, Nyxwraiths now haunt the world with impunity. Drawn to light, heat, and movement, they are both predators and omens — reminders of what the world has lost and what it must now guard against. Most Holds maintain elaborate defenses: mirrored barriers, radiant wards, and squads of trained duskguard.

Areas where Shards have failed are considered **Hollowed Zones** — cursed or barren spaces often left abandoned and forgotten.

---
## Cultural Impact

Nightfall severed the rhythms of old life.

- **Timekeeping** now varies between regions, often reset to the age of each Hold's founding.
- **Religions fractured** — some believe [[Sael]] turned away, others that mortals themselves caused the silence.
- **Language shifted**, with new idioms for resilience, sacrifice, and the memory of warmth.

Despite the darkness, life endures. Shards glow. Trade routes reopen. Lightbearers walk the night.

But always with the knowledge: the sun has not risen… and may never again.
