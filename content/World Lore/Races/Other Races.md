## Humans – *The Adaptive Thread*
> *“We did not endure. We adapted.”*

Humans thrive in every #Hold and #Shard, not because they are the strongest or most brilliant, but because they adapt faster than the world around them. [[Nightfall]] fractured old kingdoms and gave rise to new city-states, guilds, and coalitions—many human-led.  
They are the builders, the brokers, the breakers. No two communities are alike, and no single culture defines them. Their strength is in their capacity to fit to the situation surrounding them.

---
## Dwarves – *The Holdfast Legacy*
> *“We held the line while others fled.”*

Once proud of their isolation in stone keeps and deep cities, dwarves were among the first devastated by the scourge of the [[Nyxwraiths]]. Forced to abandon ancient mountain homes, they now act as builders, defenders, and architects in the #shards —gruff but indispensable.

Clannish pride remains, but they have shifted from tradition to pragmatism. Stone is still sacred. But *walls* now matter more than *halls*.

---
## Halflings – *The Hearthwarmers*
> *“The world darkened. So we lit lanterns in it.”*

Halflings responded to [[Nightfall]] with community, not conquest. Their roaming caravans often serve as vital supply lines or ensuring safe routes between settlements. Some have settled in the #Shards as beloved artisans, brewers, or quartermasters.

They are rarely leaders by title, but always remembered—carriers of song, story, and simple warmth in a world always on edge.

---
## Gnomes – *The Bright-Makers*
> **“If the sun won’t rise, we’ll make one better.”**  

Gnomes threw themselves into post-[[Nightfall]] invention. Arc-lamps, containment wards, shade barriers, and tracking tech all bear gnomish ingenuity.

Most now live embedded in larger societies as engineers or arcane designers. Others remain in small think-tanks called **glimmers**, often obsessive and borderline cult-like in their mission to rekindle the world.

---
## Dragonborn – *The Living Ember*
> *“We are what the world tried to forget.”** 

Rare and draconic in form, dragonborn often share mythic whispers with minotaurs. Some believe they are creations of [[Sael]] or [[Theryn]], or remnants of ancient progenitor dragons tied to the [[aether]].

Most dragonborn today are solitary or temple-raised. They tend to be mistaken as more beast than person—but they remember. They are not monsters.

---
## Orcs – *The Shadow’s Edge*
> *“We do not flinch from the dark. We step into it.”*

Orcs often serve as boundary-keepers—working dangerous jobs others fear, including as members of the [[Duskrunners]] as wraith-hunters and ward-masters. Their resilience is respected, but their rage is still stereotyped.

Some lean into the edge. Others fight to define themselves beyond it. Either way, they are shaped by the harshest parts of the world—and it shows.
