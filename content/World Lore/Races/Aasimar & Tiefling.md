> *“The stars shifted. The roles reversed. The world never forgets.”*

## Overview
Before [[Nightfall]], **Aasimar** were living blessings—embodied grace and divine avatars of [[Sael]]’s light. **Tieflings** were mistrusted, pushed to the margins and considered cursed for their infernal ancestry. But when the sun fell and the world turned to night, so too did society’s expectations.

Darkvision became survival. Divine beauty became a reminder of a god that did not come to save them. And the roles flipped.

Now, tieflings walk freer than they ever have—though never fully embraced. Aasimar walk as outcasts, reminders of promises broken.

---
## Tieflings – *The Unshackled*
For tieflings, Nightfall brought a strange kind of freedom. No longer vilified as demonic threats, they became useful—resilient, capable, necessary in a world built for darkness.

Some revel in this. They dye their horns in gold and walk with swagger, finally allowed to live without fear. Others remain cautious. They remember the cold stares and closed doors. Acceptance, to them, feels fragile.

Though their infernal heritage ties them to a once-feared source of power, tieflings in this age are often seen as part of the living world’s rhythm. Many follow [[Theryn]]’s pull—the change, the chaos, the freedom to move and remake oneself.

---

## Aasimar – *The Scorned Chosen*
Aasimar have suffered a true fall from grace. Once treated as divine gifts, they are now symbols of abandonment—celestial heirs of a dead god. The faithless spit in their direction. Others give them wide berth, afraid their presence will draw divine ire.

Some hide what they are. They take on false names, conceal their glow, and use no magic unless forced. Others double down—raising their voices, brandishing their wings, determined to remind the world of what was lost.

In the darkest corners of the #Holds, aasimar creches have begun to form. Secretive and ritualistic, these cloistered groups raise their kin with purpose and pride, forging them into living tributes of endurance and spiritual strength. A name in a creche is a rebirth. A path.

---
## Public Perception
- **Tieflings** are tolerated, sometimes celebrated, often side-eyed. In many places they’re “just another neighbor,” but always a half-step away from suspicion. 
- **Aasimar** face open scorn. Not systemic laws, but everyday cruelty—avoided, reviled, sometimes even blamed for [[Nightfall]]’s legacy.

---
## Adventuring
- **Tieflings** become Duskrunners for a mix of reasons—freedom, escape, adventure, or the thrill of change. Many are running from debts, mistakes, or expectations.   
- **Aasimar** seek the guild for sanctuary, for autonomy, or as a way to reclaim their purpose. The guild does not care who you are, only what you can do—and that alone makes it a kind of salvation.

Some aasimar see their mission as sacred: to walk into darkness not as forsaken children, but as vengeful light. To make Sael remember them.

---
## Names and Identity
- **Tieflings** often keep their birth names, though some choose to adopt new ones reflecting Theryn’s domains—freedom, transformation, or poetic reinvention. 
- **Aasimar** commonly take on new names when inducted into their creche. These names are ritualistically given and often replace their old identity entirely. To speak your _given_ name is a declaration of belonging. To hide it is a shield against a world that will not understand.
