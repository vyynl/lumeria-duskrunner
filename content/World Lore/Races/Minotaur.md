# The Horned Flame
> *“To bear the shape of a beast is not to be one.”*

## Overview
Rare even before [[Nightfall]], minotaurs are now among the least-understood ancestries in the world. Towering and horned, they appear to most as some strange hybrid of beast and man—part folk tale, part fascination. Yet those who know them speak of poise, discipline, and immense restraint.

Unlike the chaotic legends that once framed them as labyrinth-bound monsters, the minotaurs of the current age walk a different path: one of *devotion*, *structure*, and *balance*.

They are rare not because they are secretive, but because so few remain. Those who tend to walk under the sun’s memory, guided by something greater than themselves.

---
## Origins and Rarity
Minotaurs are not a nation. There is no homeland, no singular culture. Some speculate they share ancestry with dragonborn—another rare, hybrid race shaped by ancient forces—but no confirmed link exists. What is known is this:
- Minotaurs are not born accidentally. They appear with purpose.
- Since [[Nightfall]], most have been accepted into society with cautious curiosity. Most people have never seen one—let alone spoken to one—but recognize their silhouette in stories and street whispers. In cities, they draw eyes like comets: quietly, irresistibly.

---
## Monastic Culture – *The Horned Orders*
Though minotaurs are not numerous enough to form a culture in the traditional sense, many are raised in isolated monastic groups devoted to [[Sael]]—not as a god of warmth or power, but of *discipline through radiance*.

These orders (often referred to as **The Horned Orders**, though each sect bears its own name) teach that rage is not wild—it is a *forge*. Through spiritual focus, physical training, and sacred rites, minotaurs learn to channel what others call “fury” into *radiant action*. For many, this is literal: their rage is a divine expression of [[Sael]]’s will burning through muscle and mind.

> *"Your body is a vessel. The flame flows through it. Let it burn clean."*

---
## Public Perception
To most, minotaurs are an oddity. Not feared, exactly—but gawked at. Travelers may ask to touch their horns, or whisper assumptions about their “animal instincts.” In truth, minotaurs often show more control than the ones who mock them.

Still, some lean into the spectacle, playing the part for coin or leverage. Others stay silent. A few simply leave.

---
## Dual Natures
Minotaurs walk a line between **inner beast** and **structured being**. Some see their strength as sacred—a thing to wield with clarity. Others embrace their raw nature fully, letting instinct guide them.

Neither path is wrong. Both burn with meaning.

---
## Adventuring
Minotaurs rarely wander without reason. Most who join the [[Duskrunners]] do so on their own divine mission—sent by their order to carry [[Sael]]’s light into the shadowed wilds. Others leave in search of purpose, exile, or personal revelation.

Regardless of reason, a minotaur Duskrunner is never a passive presence. They walk as mountain-sparks: slow to burn, impossible to ignore.

---
## Names and Identity
Minotaurs rarely keep birth names. Instead, they are **named by their order**—a calling more than a label. These names often evoke trials passed or virtues pursued:  

> *Ashbreaker, Still-Horn, Dawnward, Flame-Root, Silence-of-Stone.*

A name is earned. A name is worn.
