# The Dwellers of the Deep
> *"We who remember the dark, and chose to walk in light.*
## Overview
Once divided by blood, border, and belief, the peoples of the Underdark have found new identity in a shared truth: **they survived**. When the [[Nyxwraiths]] plagued the tunnels of their home, the different races set aside past hostilities and rose to the surface—a world once alien and hostile now become their only hope.

In the years following [[Nightfall]], these once-fractured peoples have come to call themselves **Kheltari**—a name meaning *those who remember the stone*. Though distinctions of heritage persist, the name reflects a cultural unity built on shared loss, migration, and transformation.

---
## Appearance and Shared Traits
While Kheltari subgroups retain distinct appearances—drow grace, duergar stoicism, gnome precision—all share ancestry adapted to darkness: keen darkvision, comfort in confined spaces, and a silent grace born from millennia underground.

To outsiders, differences blur. The surface tongue often collapses Kheltari identity into a single stereotype. But within their own communities, traditions remain proudly diverse.

---
## Culture and Outlook
Among the Kheltari, one truth runs deep: the past should be remembered, but not all must return to it.
- **Elders** seek to preserve ancestral languages, old rituals, and songs carved in stone. Many feel uneasy in the open air, clinging to structure and tradition.
- **Youths**, born in the time of artificial suns and urban night, often seek motion and meaning. To them, [[Nightfall]] is not a curse, but an invitation—freedom from tunnels, from hiding, from limits.

This generational divide sparks tension, but also gives rise to rich culture: festivals combining ancient rites with city lights, hybrid names, and new philosophies forged in the space between memory and motion.

---
## Life in the Shards
Most Kheltari live in tight-knit districts shaped by early laws that confined them to the outskirts. Some cities maintain these divisions; others have blurred them with time. Community bonds run deep, shaped by shared struggle and mutual aid.

Their darkvision and resilience make them indispensable in many trades, especially where danger walks in shadow. Still, appreciation and prejudice often walk hand in hand—rarely open hostility, but always a hint of distance.

---
## Adventuring
For many Kheltari, adventuring is not rebellion—it is fulfillment. They find the call of freedom in the echoing silence of the surface night.

Some seek to join the [[Duskrunners]] to escape memory. Others, to prove worth. But most go simply because they can—because the stars were once myths, and now they shine on every path.
