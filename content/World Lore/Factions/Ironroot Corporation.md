The Ironroot Corporation is the most powerful economic entity within the shard of Wellspring and one of the few organizations with the infrastructure and audacity to claim jurisdiction over the lands beyond the artificial light. Their branding speaks of innovation, renewal, and survival—but their boots tend to leave deep prints.

Ironroot is the primary exporter of **living wood**, a rare material coaxed from magically accelerated trees and harvested to fuel Wellspring’s growth. Their timber is known for its strength, resilience, and regenerative properties—vital in repairing the ever-weathered skeletons of the shard’s districts.

But where their lumber grows, so does their influence.

---
## Public Image

To the average shard-born citizen, Ironroot is a necessary constant: builders of homes, funders of public lightworks, employers of thousands. Their symbols are etched into bricks, crates, and lampposts. Their name appears on pay slips, and their slogans hang in schoolhouses and recruitment halls. They offer safety, prosperity, and the promise of stability in a world that no longer sees the sun.

They also have a reputation for doing whatever it takes to keep those promises intact.

---
## Known Operations

- Exclusive control of Wellspring’s **living wood harvest zones**
- **Contract employment** of mercenary groups such as the [[Bronze Gauntlet]]
- **Containment and research** of arcane or Aether-sensitive materials
- Deployment of **private enforcement teams** to stabilize “uncooperative” districts

While their official partnership with the [[Duskrunners]] is publicly maintained as a neutral transaction of labor and logistics, many note the disproportionate number of high-priority guild missions that lead back to Ironroot interests.

---
## Internal Culture

Ironroot promotes a culture of **loyalty**, **efficiency**, and **opportunity**—at least on paper. Advancement is possible, but costly. Silence is encouraged. Field agents and logistics officers often operate under vague orders and wide latitude, particularly in outer districts where watchful eyes grow scarce.

The corporation prides itself on being **self-governing**—employing its own auditors, internal security, and legal teams. Appeals rarely go beyond the company’s walls.

---
## Criticism and Allegations

Though Ironroot maintains a spotless public front, stories slip through:

- **Disappearances** in low-income districts like [[Hearthgrove]]
- Suppression of magical anomalies and unlicensed casters
- Bribery of watch officers, forced relocations, and sealed “containment accidents”

Critics call it a machine.  
Supporters call it civilization.

The truth, as always, lies somewhere between timber and tyranny.
