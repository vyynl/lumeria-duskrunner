## Serious Injury

When a character fails their 3rd death saving throw, they become **unconscious and unresponsive** until the party takes a rest. Upon resting, the character is revived with a permanent **Serious Injury** debuff reflective of their injury's nature.

> [!note]+
> **Serious Injuries** are impactful but not game-breaking, such as:
> - Reduced movement speed (-5 ft.) from a leg injury.
> - Decreased effective range with bows or crossbows from an eye injury.
> 
> These injuries can eventually be healed, but require significant time, resources, and specialized expertise typically unavailable to low-level adventurers.

The character recovers at **half HP** after a short rest or **full HP** after a long rest, along with the associated *Serious Injury*.

> [!danger]+
> Depending on injury severity, characters can sustain only **1–2 active Serious Injuries**. Accumulating more risks **permanent death**, barring rare and expensive revivification options within Lumeria.

## Modified Attack of Opportunity

To encourage dynamic positioning and movement in combat, the Attack of Opportunity rules are adjusted:

> [!quote]+ Definition
> If a creature leaves melee range while surrounded by **3 or more aggressive opponents**, **half of those opponents (rounded down)** may immediately use their reactions to make a melee attack roll against that creature.

## Rolling with Emphasis

When rolling a check **with emphasis**, roll **2d20** and select the result farthest from **10**.

> [!info]+ Rulings
> 
> - Advantage or disadvantage **do not** apply to Emphasis rolls, only proficiency and modifiers.
>     

---

## Critical Skill Checks

Skill checks now include the below rules for achieving critical outcomes, providing more frequent dramatic results for rolls outside a 1 or 20:

> [!quote]+ Definition
> A skill check results in a **Critical Success** or **Critical Failure** when:
> - A natural **20** or **1** is rolled, **or**
> - The check exceeds or fails the DC by **10 or more**.
